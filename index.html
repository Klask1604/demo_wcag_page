How to Recognize Reserved Spaces

All accessible parking spaces are clearly marked with the international wheelchair symbol painted in blue on white background. Each space is 3.6 meters wide with an additional 1.2-meter access aisle marked with diagonal lines. Look for the blue and white signs mounted at eye level that display the wheelchair symbol and "Reserved for Disabled" text in multiple languages.

Route to Terminal Entrance

The pathway from all accessible parking areas to terminal entrances features smooth, level surfaces with tactile guidance strips for visually impaired passengers. All routes are well-lit with emergency call buttons positioned every 50 meters. The maximum gradient on any pathway is 1:20, ensuring easy wheelchair navigation.

Accessibility Features

All routes to terminal entrances are fully accessible with no level differences or steps. Where elevation changes are necessary, gentle ramps with handrails on both sides are provided. Rest areas with benches are positioned every 30 meters along longer pathways. Weather protection is available through covered walkways in all terminal approach areas.

Payment Methods

Payment stations are located at each parking area exit with multilingual interfaces and wheelchair-accessible height positioning.

Priority Boarding Process

Passengers requiring accessibility assistance are invited to board 15 minutes before general boarding begins. This allows adequate time for proper seating, equipment storage, and safety briefings without rushing. Gate agents will personally escort passengers and coordinate with cabin crew for seamless boarding.

Equipment Handling

Personal wheelchairs and mobility devices are handled with special care by trained ground crew. Wheelchairs can be gate-checked and returned immediately upon arrival, or stored in the cargo hold with protective covering. Battery-powered devices follow specific safety protocols with proper documentation and secure storage.

Priority Boarding

Wheelchair Storage Options

Gate-check service allows wheelchairs to be returned immediately upon arrival. Cargo storage includes protective covering and secure positioning. Battery removal and documentation handled by certified staff. Replacement wheelchairs available during flight if needed.

Battery-Powered Equipment

Lithium batteries must be removed and carried in cabin. Lead-acid batteries can remain installed with proper terminals protection. All procedures follow IATA dangerous goods regulations. Staff provide battery removal assistance if needed.

Boarding Methods

Jet Bridge Boarding

Level boarding through jet bridges with adjustable height and width. No steps or elevation changes. Direct wheelchair access to aircraft door.

Ambulift Boarding

Hydraulic lift platform for aircraft without jet bridges. Enclosed cabin with climate control. Smooth elevation to aircraft door level.

Ramp Boarding

Gentle-gradient ramps for smaller aircraft. Non-slip surfaces with handrails. Staff assistance available throughout the boarding process.

Boarding Assistance Team

Each boarding operation includes a dedicated assistance team: gate agent for coordination, ground crew for equipment handling, and cabin crew for onboard assistance. Teams are trained in proper lifting techniques, equipment operation, and emergency procedures. Communication between team members ensures smooth passenger transfer from gate to seat.

Staff Training & Clear Communication

Staff members undergo rigorous training programs that emphasize the importance of clear articulation, appropriate speaking pace, and always facing the passenger when speaking. They understand the critical importance of not raising their voice unnecessarily, as this can be perceived as condescending or patronizing. Instead, they focus on articulating words clearly and using descriptive language that paints a vivid and accurate picture of the surroundings, procedures, and available services. This training ensures that no passenger can be refused service or assistance because of their disability or mobility challenges, with only security-related exceptions as permitted by national or international regulations.

Comprehensive Step-by-Step Process Guidance

European Union airport regulations mandate that staff offer to verbally introduce themselves and clearly describe each step of the check-in process from start to finish, ensuring transparency and accessibility for all passengers with disabilities. This comprehensive approach includes announcing their name, professional role, and providing detailed explanations of what they will be doing throughout the entire interaction process. The systematic verbal guidance eliminates uncertainty and anxiety often experienced by visually impaired travelers, creating a supportive environment where passengers can navigate the check-in process with confidence and dignity.

Personal Assistance & Gate Escort Services

Visually impaired passengers can request comprehensive personal assistance directly at any check-in counter, receiving dedicated support that will accompany them throughout their entire airport journey, from check-in completion all the way to the boarding gate and aircraft boarding process. This essential service is provided completely free of charge in accordance with European Union regulations, does not require advance booking or special arrangements, though advance notice is strongly recommended during peak travel periods to ensure optimal service delivery and staffing availability.

Companion & Guide Dog Access Policy

Companions and certified guide dogs are welcome and granted unrestricted access at all check-in counters throughout the airport terminal, in full compliance with European Union accessibility regulations that guarantee equal access to air transport services. Adequate space is specifically designed and maintained to accommodate guide dogs comfortably beside or behind the counter area, ensuring the animal remains close to their handler throughout the entire check-in process while maintaining proper working conditions for the service animal. Check-in counter areas feature sufficient space for companions to stand alongside passengers and provide necessary assistance as needed.

Procedure for granting assistance

Ticket Purchase Assistance Request

When purchasing the ticket, a disabled person can tick the dedicated option to receive assistance and mention whether they are accompanied by a guide dog, to ensure that the animal is allowed on board the aircraft. This advance notification allows airport staff to coordinate appropriate assistance services and ensure guide dog accommodation is properly arranged for the travel experience.

Universal Staff Responsibility

Disabled passengers can choose not to explicitly request assistance when purchasing the ticket. However, airport employees whether at check-in counters, security control, duty-free shops, restaurants or in other areas have the responsibility to recognize passengers who may need assistance and proactively offer appropriate support services throughout their airport experience, ensuring no passenger requiring assistance goes without proper care.
